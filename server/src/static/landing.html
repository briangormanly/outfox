Outfox

Introduction

Outfox is an open-source educational platform that encourages the betterment of its users through the dissemination of instructive content. Unlike privately accessible platforms like Blackboard and Moodle, Outfox provides all users with the opportunity to access all available content, as well as to make meaningful contributions. As a platform that is user-centric, the user has the power to find groups that interest them, join groups, and add resources to it in their own personal view as they see fit. Everyone can interact with the content and make their own changes, make comments, and tailor their educational experience to what works best for them

Background

Outfox is a Marist College capping project whose production began in the Fall of 2020. Every year since then, it has been passed down and perfected by students of following classes.

Mission

Learning is at our core, we do it everyday. The artifacts of what we learn, our notes, papers, links we find, videos we watch, blogs we read, spreadsheets, test results, assignments, books, and our lifetime of interactions with teachers and mentors are spread across the physical and digital world.

When we graduate, finish an online course, complete a training seminar, attend a conference or even just switch to a new method of managing our notes and documents we lose something, pieces of our hard work scattered, unorganized, out of reach and eventually lost. Often years later we find ourselves frustrated and retracing our steps though a past learning or research journey only to realize a crucial piece cannot be located or reconstructed.

We need a single, open, collaborative and forever free environment where we can conduct our learning, research our ideas, and remember all the lessons we learn, forever.
